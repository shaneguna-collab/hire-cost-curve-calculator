Hire a clinician now and the wage doesn't sit still. Every year they move up the award bands and the new rates phase in underneath them. Here's the same person, same role, costing more each year — set the inputs and watch the curve.

Two forces stack, every year

The clinician moves up the experience bands (1st year, 2nd–3rd, 4th–6th, 7th year+), and the award rates phase in from 1 October 2026 to 2030. The cost rises faster than either on its own, and it jumps at the band crossings — around year 4 and year 7.

Same person, same role, more money

Nothing here assumes a promotion or a pay negotiation. This is the floor rising under a clinician who simply keeps showing up — the cost an owner signs on to without a single decision after the hire.

What is locked, and what is your call

The base is the published 1 October 2026 rate with the confirmed 4.75% review applied. The 2028–2030 steps are interpolated between the Commission's two published figures; anything past 4.75% is your inflation assumption. Figures are base wage plus 12% super — leave loading, penalties and bonus sit on top.
